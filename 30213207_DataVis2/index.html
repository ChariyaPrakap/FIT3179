How has mental illnesses impacted the world?

In 2017, global mental health rates continued to be a growing concern.

These conditions led to a loss in productivity, increased healthcare costs, and

significant personal suffering. Stigma persisted, hindering timely treatment and support.

However, the year also saw a growing awareness of these issues,

fostering hope for better mental health outcomes.

Anxiety Disorder

Australia is ranked 7TH in the global anxiety rate ranking.

Here are some ways how anxiety affects university students :

Academic Performance

DIfficulty concentrating

Anxiety can make it challenging to focus on lectures, study materials, and assignments.

Poor memory retention

High anxiety levels can impair memory and

information recall during exams or presentations.

Time Management

Procrastination

Anxiety can lead to a tendency to delay assignments and projects,

causing last-minute rushes and subpar work.

Impaired Cognitive function

Reduced problem-solving abilities

High anxiety levels can impair cognitive functions, making it

difficult to solve complex problems or think critically

Depression In Selected Countries (2000-2017)

There are several reasons as to why a country's

depression rate is higher than the other :

1. Cultural stigma :

Cultural attitudes towards mental health and stigma surrounded it can

significantly impact depression rates as there may be greater reluctance

to seek help for such issues, contributing to higher depression rates .

2. Income Inequality & Unemployment

Such factors can contribute to inreased stress and depression

rates as it often leads to disparities in access to resources and opportunities.

3. Academic or Work Pressure

In some countries, the cultural emphasis on academic or career success can

contribute to stress and depression, especially among students and professionals.

Depression Rate In Different University Courses

Top 3 Courses with Highest Depression Rates

1. Computer Science

Very demanding course due to complex nature of subject matter

and long hours spent on coding and problem-solving. The pressure

to keep up with rapidly evolving technology can also be a source of stress.

Factors : The fear of falling behind in a fast-paced field and the high expectations in the tech industry

2. Medical

Highly demanding course which requires extensive study hours, clinical responsibilities,

and a commitment to patients' well-being. The pressure to excel and the emotional

demands of the medical field can be significant sources of stress.

Factors : Emotional toll of dealing with illness, patient suffering, and high-stress situations

3. Law

Heavy workload, competitive atmosphere, and high expectations.

The pressure to succeed in exams, internships, and eventually the legal profession

can lead to increased stress.

Factors : High-stakes nature of legal work, long hours, and the competitive job market
